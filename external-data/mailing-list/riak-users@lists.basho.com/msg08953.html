On Tue, Oct 9, 2012 at 9:46 AM, Tomas Charvat <t...@excello.cz> wrote:

>  We have manage to fix mentioned segfault byt disabling sysfs restriction
> in hardened Gentoo kernel.
> It has fixed segfault issues.
> Sysfs/debugfs restriction
>
> *Configuration option: CONFIG_GRKERNSEC_SYSFS_RESTRICT*
> If you say Y here, sysfs (the pseudo-filesystem mounted at /sys) and any
> filesystem normally mounted under it (e.g. debugfs) will only be accessible
> by root. These filesystems generally provide access to hardware and debug
> information that isn't appropriate for unprivileged users of the system.
> Sysfs and debugfs have also become a large source of new vulnerabilities,
> ranging from infoleaks to local compromise.
>
> There has been very little oversight with an eye toward security involved
> in adding new exporters of information to these filesystems, so their use
> is discouraged.
>
> This option is equivalent to a chmod 0700 of the mount paths.

> Tomas
