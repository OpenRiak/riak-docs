On Sat, Nov 23, 2013 at 12:07 PM, John Daily  wrote:

&gt; allow\_mult=true is the recommended approach for addressing conflict 
&gt; resolution; when multiple writes to the same object are received and vector 
&gt; clocks cannot determine a causal history, all copies of the writes are 
&gt; retained for future merging/selection by the application. We are planning to 
&gt; make this the default setting for Riak 2.0.
&gt; A couple of blog posts (pardon me for referencing my own content):
&gt; https://basho.com/understanding-riaks-configurable-behaviors-part-1/
&gt; https://basho.com/clocks-are-bad-or-welcome-to-distributed-systems/
&gt; I imagine that the presence of these multiple copies (aka “siblings”) is what 
&gt; is breaking legacy search, but that’s purely guesswork on my part.
&gt; -John
