&gt; Rohit,

&gt; 

&gt; My apologies for the delayed reply. Too many conflicting demands on
&gt; my time the past two weeks.
&gt; 

&gt; I reviewed the riak-debug package you shared. I also discussed its
&gt; contents with other Riak developers.
&gt; 

&gt; There does not appear to be anything unexpected. The anti\_entropy
&gt; bloat is due to the bitcask backend not actively communicating TTL
&gt; expirations to AAE. This is a known issue. Similarly, bitcask is not
&gt; communicating TTL expirations to solr. (The leveldb backend recently
&gt; added expiry / TTL. And it fails the same way in this scenario as
&gt; bitcask.)
&gt; 

&gt; We have engineering designs in the works that will eventually correct
&gt; this situation. But the designs do not translate to code that you can
&gt; use today. My apologies.

&gt; 

&gt; --&gt; The 100% accurate approach today is to disable bitcask's TTL and
&gt; create external jobs that prune your data via Delete operations. Yes,
&gt; this is going to create a bunch of extra disk operations. But I am
&gt; being honest.
&gt; 

&gt; --&gt; You could reduce only the anti\_entropy disk usage by changing the
&gt; "anti\_entropy" setting in riak.conf from "active" to "passive". But
&gt; this does nothing for solr.
