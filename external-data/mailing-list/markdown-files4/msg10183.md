&gt; &gt; That warning comes from the shell script wrapper that runs starts / stops
&gt; &gt; Riak, so if the shell is reporting a ulimit lower than 4096 you'll get
&gt; that
&gt; &gt; warning. A good way to test for sure what ulimit Riak is seeing is to do
&gt; &gt; the following:
&gt; &gt;
&gt; &gt; \* do a riak console to start in interactive mode
&gt; &gt; \* when startup finishes, type os:cmd('ulimit -n').
&gt; &gt;
&gt; &gt; You should see something like:
&gt; &gt;
&gt; &gt; (dev1@127.0.0.1)1&gt; os:cmd('ulimit -n').
&gt; &gt; "16384\n"
&gt; &gt; (dev1@127.0.0.1)2&gt;
&gt; &gt;
&gt; &gt; If it indeed prints out the correct ulimit, you can easily modify the
&gt; &gt; riakscript to not warn you.
&gt; &gt;
&gt; &gt; -Jared
