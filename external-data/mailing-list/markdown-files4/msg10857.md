Today, all my Riak-related tests have been failing. I tracked down the
reason why: many calls to riak.solr.search() are returning a 'num\_found'
that is greater than the actual number of documents they find.

Here's an example:

&gt;&gt;&gt; riak.solr.search('test\_riak-search', 'terms:plasma\|en', count=10)
{'max\_score': 0.353553, 'num\_found': 2, 'docs': [{'segments': '1 17 172
1725', 'terms': 'sun|en miasma|en of|en incandescent|en plasma|en', u'id':
'uuid-1725ca7400ba483e96fec68f92637198', 'subsets': '\_\_all\_\_'}]}

I have also seen a case, after I delete my test data, where I search and
get \_zero\_ results with a num\_found of 2:

&gt;&gt;&gt; riak.solr.search('test\_riak-search', 'terms:plasma\|en', count=10)
{'max\_score': 0.353553, 'num\_found': 2, 'docs': []}

How is this happening? Is 'num\_found' supposed to be an accurate count?
