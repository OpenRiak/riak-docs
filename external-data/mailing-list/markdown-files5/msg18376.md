Hi Steve,

Many thanks for your informative and comforting post. The program you're 
contemplating sounds sensible and, indeed, promising. 

Successful implementation and operation of Riak among enterprise users is in 
everyone's interest. Erlang Solutions has an excellent track record in support 
of Erlang. No doubt it would be a fine champion and conservator of open source 
Riak.

As an army-of-one-developer, with big dreams of an Erlang-based product, I do 
have one concern. 

I'm totally dependent on web-based documentation, tutorials, and the good 
graces of folks on the mailing lists. As much as I'd like to attend Erlang 
Solutions conferences and training sessions, they're priced far beyond my 
budget. 

So, I do hope that in your support of Riak you don't forget that there are 
indie developers with considerable interest in Erlang and Riak who, lacking 
corporate backing and travel allowances, simply can't afford your enterprise 
offerings.

Beyond that, you have my vote and support in anyway within my means.

All the best,

Lloyd
Writersglen Publications

&gt; I'm from Erlang Solutions. Just to clarify where things are at the moment. 
&gt; The company Basho Technologies still exists - it hasn't filed for bankruptcy 
&gt; yet - although we expect it to. There is no grave just yet. We are creditors 
&gt; and shareholders so we will be notified when/if they do file for bankruptcy. 
&gt; Assuming it goes into liquidation, the receiver will sell whatever assets can 
&gt; be liquidated in order to pay the creditors. Registered names like Riak are 
&gt; things which can be sold... so we will see. it will probably take a long time 
&gt; before Basho is truly dead and buried.
&gt; 
&gt; I've asked our CEO Stuart Whitfield (who happens to be a lawyer) to look at 
&gt; the legal questions and give a more detailed view.
&gt; 
&gt; As Russell correctly points out existing customers are the priority. In 
&gt; terms of our thoughts of this situation, our priority is to ensure enterprise 
&gt; customers have continuity of support since they are the ones most likely to 
&gt; abandon Riak as it will be a forced migration situation if there is no 
&gt; support. I expect to launch a paid support service very shortly - if 
&gt; you're interested, let me know.
&gt; 
&gt; In terms of Riak software, the open source software is safe since it is open 
&gt; sourced under a friendly licence. The situation with the code for the 
&gt; enterprise version is not clear yet (highly dependent who/if it is bought in 
&gt; the fire sale since it is closed source) - although we aim, one way or 
&gt; another, to have an open source data centre replication layer. Not sure who 
&gt; is using the other functionality like super clusters in the the enterprise 
&gt; version - ping me if you are.
&gt; 
&gt; In terms of the products, KV is the priority for support followed by CS/S2. 
&gt; There aren't many people using TS so it will probably be community support 
&gt; for the code. I already have some interest from a former developer to move 
&gt; TS forward - if others want to work on TS I can help co-ordinate in the 
&gt; interim.
&gt; 
&gt; In terms of evolving the code we're happy to co-ordinate a community and help 
&gt; steer the future direction for the product. We are looking whether this 
&gt; should be done through a similar model to the Linux foundation.
&gt; 
&gt; For major feature development, our thoughts are some kind of crowd funding 
&gt; model for functionality which requires significant development effort - Basho 
&gt; funded this activity through support and being closed source which doesnt 
&gt; really work in a completely open source model. Our goal would be to have 
&gt; just the open source version and no closed source code. Where possible we 
&gt; wouldnt want customers to have closed source forks.
&gt; 
&gt; We definitely see Riak has a future and want to focus on getting back to 
&gt; basics of a product which is high performance and does it well - before 
&gt; adding lots of new functionality. For example ensure there is a clean 
&gt; demarcation between the data layer and the control layer - this should make 
&gt; it easier to create new storage types and change the replication layer. Right 
&gt; now for those of Enterprise, migration back to Open Source is not straight 
&gt; forward because of this.
&gt; 
&gt; If you are planning to set up groups etc, please let me know and our 
&gt; marketing group can help you establish the community.
