On Wed, Aug 20, 2014 at 12:24 AM, Sean Cribbs  wrote:

&gt; Alex,
&gt;
&gt; Assuming you've already made your bucket-type with "map" as the
&gt; datatype, then "bucket.new()" will return you a Map instead of a
&gt; RiakObject. Translating your example above:
&gt;
&gt; key = bucket.new('lionelmessi')
&gt; key.registers['name'].assign('Messi')
&gt; key.registers['team'].assign('Barcelona')
&gt; key.counters['number'].increment(10)
&gt; key.store()
&gt;
&gt; Note that because Maps are based on mutation operations and not
&gt; replacing the value with new ones, you can later do this without
&gt; setting the entire value:
&gt;
&gt; key.counters['number'].increment(1)
&gt; key.store()
&gt;
&gt; This will also change your searches, however, in that the fields will
&gt; be suffixed with the embedded type you are using:
&gt;
&gt; r = client.fulltext\\_search('ix\\_footballers', 'team\\_register:Barcelona')
&gt;
&gt; Hope that helps!
&gt;
