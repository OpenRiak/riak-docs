&gt; On Sat, Jun 12, 2010 at 4:00 PM, Dan Reverri  wrote:
&gt; &gt; Hi Jason,
&gt; &gt; Regarding rack-aware distribution, at this time Riak is not rack-aware.
&gt; I've
&gt; &gt; filed a request in Bugzilla for this feature:
&gt; &gt; http://issues.basho.com/show\\_bug.cgi?id=245
&gt; &gt; Regarding general replica distribution, Riak guarantees that replicas
&gt; will
&gt; &gt; be placed on at least N-1 distinct nodes when physically possible\\*. This
&gt; &gt; means for N=3, replicas will be placed on at least 2 distinct nodes. In
&gt; most
&gt; &gt; cases replicas will be placed on N distinct nodes but there are some
&gt; &gt; degenerate cases where replicas are only placed on 2 nodes. More
&gt; information
&gt; &gt; can be found here:
&gt; &gt; http://issues.basho.com/show\\_bug.cgi?id=228
&gt; &gt; \\*When physically possible refers to having at least N physical nodes
&gt; &gt; available. Meaning you can't place 3 replicas on distinct nodes if you
&gt; only
&gt; &gt; have 2 nodes.

&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; -------------------------
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; Riak 0.11.0 Release Notes
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; -------------------------
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; Bitcask has arrived as the new default backend for Riak. Bitcask
&gt; is
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; Basho's
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; new key/value store that should provide good balanced performance
&gt; out
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; of the box on most systems. Read more about it from the initial
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; announcement here http://blog.basho.com/2010/04/27/hello,-bitcask/
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; Users that wish to upgrade from another backend will need to backup
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; their
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; cluster (using riak-admin) before changing the backend in
&gt; app.config,
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; restart
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; riak and restore from the backup into bitcask.
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; The protocol buffers client (PBC) interface has been enhanced to
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; add map/reduce support and access to simple bucket properties. The
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; erlang and python clients have been updated accordingly.
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; Put operations using the local erlang client that request a
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; returnbody
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; have received a performance enhancement. Internally the put
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; operation
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; now returns the body directly, so an additional get is no longer
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; required. The PBC and HTTP interfaces have been updated to use
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; this new mechanism.
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; Enhancements
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; --------
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; 58 - provide default content-type of application/octet-stream if
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; none
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; present in the object.
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; 74 - create ring directory if non-existant
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; 142 - riak-admin leave restored
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; Bugs Fixed
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; ----------
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; 35 - {error, blah} messages are now passed on to javascript
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; map/reduce
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; jobs
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; 104 - missing bucket/key into map/reduce job crashed it. Fixed.
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; 193 - list keys sometimes uses downed nodes. Fixed.
&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt;&gt; &gt; 208 - deleting a key with a post-commit hook set crashed riak.
&gt; Fixed.
