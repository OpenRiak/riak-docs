On Tue, Jan 13, 2015 at 9:51 PM, Sean Cribbs  wrote:

&gt; The version of Solr bundled with Riak 2.0.4 is 4.7.0.
&gt;
&gt; I'm not sure what process would be required to bundle tokenizers and
&gt; analyzers, but I suspect it would be similar to how you deploy them with
&gt; stock Solr. Some tweaks may need to be made to load customizations.
&gt;
&gt; You cannot upgrade the Solr version by itself because we build a small
&gt; amount of custom code into Solr and we want to validate each upgrade before
&gt; shipping it. Since it uses some Solr-internal interfaces, we can't
&gt; guarantee that our customizations won't break on upgrade.
&gt;
&gt; The search is "planned" on the Riak side (which shards/cores to query,
&gt; etc) but executed by Distributed Solr. Search performance is good; Ryan
&gt; Zezeski benchmarked it extensively in its initial development. However, the
&gt; primary benefit of using Riak's Solr integration is the automatic
&gt; synchronization of your key-value data with the search indexes. As
&gt; key-value data gets written, handed off, or repaired, your search index is
&gt; automatically kept up-to-date via the same mechanism that keeps individual
&gt; replicas of the originals in sync.
