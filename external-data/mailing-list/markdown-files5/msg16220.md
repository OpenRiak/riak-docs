I am very happy to read this. Thanks to all involved, and best of luck to
Sean as project maintainer.

On Friday, June 5, 2015, Matthew Brender  wrote:

&gt; Hey Community,
&gt;
&gt; The team at Basho is happy to announce we are extending Webmachine
&gt; into its own organization on GitHub:
&gt;
&gt; https://github.com/webmachine/webmachine
&gt;
&gt; The primary owners will be informal to start with Sean Cribbs leading
&gt; the change. Please reach out to him to coordinate your involvement
&gt; going forward. I’ll stay on as a co-owner to coordinate inside Basho.
&gt;
&gt; You’ll notice the original Webmachine repo is owned by the
&gt; organization and we’ve forked a copy back to Basho. All your existing
&gt; forks will work just fine, though you may need to recreate your
&gt; upstream (if you were pushing directly to the Basho repo):
&gt;
&gt; ➜ webmachine git:(develop) git remote -v
&gt; upstream https://github.com/basho/webmachine.git (fetch)
&gt; upstream https://github.com/basho/webmachine.git (push)
&gt; ➜ webmachine git:(develop) git remote set-url upstream
&gt; https://github.com/webmachine/webmachine.git
&gt; https://github.com/basho/webmachine.git
&gt; ➜ webmachine git:(develop) git remote -v
&gt; upstream https://github.com/webmachine/webmachine.git (fetch)
&gt; upstream https://github.com/webmachine/webmachine.git (push)
&gt;
&gt;
&gt; A big thanks is due to Sean Cribbs, Jon Meredith and Heather McKelvey
&gt; for helping this move along. We all believe a larger maintainer base
&gt; on Webmachine will continue to improve our community as a whole.
&gt;
&gt; Please continue to share this throughout the community.
&gt;
&gt; Best,
