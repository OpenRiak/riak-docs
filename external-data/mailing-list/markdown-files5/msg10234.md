On 24 February 2013 18:59, Brian Roach  wrote:

&gt;
&gt; One issue that immediately stands out:
&gt;
&gt; &gt; &gt;&gt;&gt;
&gt; &gt; Bucket bucket = sharedClient.createBucket("accounts1").execute();
&gt; &gt; bucket.store(a.getId().toString(),
&gt; mapper.writeValueAsString(a)).execute();
&gt; &gt; &gt;&gt;&gt;
&gt;
&gt; 'CreateBucket()' is doing a fetch of the bucket properties and then
&gt; storing them back to the cluster when 'execute()' is called.
&gt;
&gt; You want to fetch the bucket once then pass around the reference to it, or
&gt; at the very least use:
&gt; fetchBucket("accounts1").lazyLoadBucketProperties().execute();
&gt; Inside your threads.
&gt;
&gt; The only time you ever want to use createBucket() is when you want to
&gt; modify the bucket properties.
&gt;
&gt; - Roach
