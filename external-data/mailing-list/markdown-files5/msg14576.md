Is the requirement for having AAE enabled now removed for strong consistency?

On Mon, Jul 28, 2014 at 4:55 PM, Joseph Blomstedt  wrote:
&gt; This means the consistency sub-system is not enabled/active. You can
&gt; verify this with the output of `riak-admin ensemble-status`.
&gt;
&gt; To enable strong consistency you must:
&gt;
&gt; 1) Set 'strong\\_consistency = on' in riak.conf.
&gt; 2) Have at least a 3 node cluster.
&gt;
&gt; You can address #2 by setting up 3+ local developer nodes as detailed
&gt; in the 5 minute tutorial:
&gt; http://docs.basho.com/riak/2.0.0/quickstart
&gt;
&gt; Alternatively, you can override the need for 3 nodes and use 1 node.
&gt;
&gt; To do that,
&gt;
&gt; 1) Run 'riak attach' to attach to your Riak's node console
&gt; 2) Enter (including the period): riak\\_ensemble\\_manager:enable().
&gt; 3) Enter (including the period): riak\\_core\\_ring\\_manager:force\\_update().
&gt; 3) Detach from the console using: Ctrl-C a
&gt;
&gt; After either approach, re-check `riak-admin ensemble-status`. It may
&gt; take up to a minute for the consistency sub-system to be enabled.
&gt;
&gt; If you haven't already, please take a look at the temporary (until we
&gt; finish updating docs.basho.com) strong consistency related
&gt; documentation (linked from the 2.0 RC1 release notes) here:
&gt; https://github.com/basho/riak\\_ensemble/blob/wip/riak-2.0-user-docs/riak\\_consistent\\_user\\_docs.md
&gt;
&gt; Regards,
&gt; Joe
