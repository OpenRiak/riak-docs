&gt; My first post to the list.
&gt;
&gt; I want to store 20M of bounded FIFO queues (each queue has at most 50
&gt; items, 1KB each). Distribution: 80% of updates at 20% of queues.
&gt;
&gt; Insertion rate: at most 5K per second, random; 50M per day.
&gt; Reading rate: at most 10K per second, each read reads all 50 items.
&gt; Once inserted items are not updated. Oldest at each queue is discarded so
&gt; that queue length &lt;= 50.
&gt;
&gt; How much hardware I might need? From my calculations I need at least 1000
&gt; HDDs assuming that seek time is at 7ms (high end). And that is unreal
&gt; number.
&gt;
&gt; For this type of load (small random reads/writes) SSD seems to be a
&gt; perfect match.
&gt;
&gt; I'm looking at the following configuration:
&gt; 5 of OCZ 512GB Vertex 4 drives at 5 64GB RAM boxes.
&gt; Riak cluster with N=3.
&gt;
&gt; I'm going for cheaper drives because I assume that Riak will allow me to
&gt; replace a node if one of disks suddenly dies.
