&gt;&gt; On Sat, Mar 22, 2014 at 5:33 AM, Matthew Von-Maszewski
&gt;&gt;  wrote:
&gt;&gt;&gt; Leveldb, as written by Google, does not actively clean up delete 
&gt;&gt;&gt; "tombstones" or prior data records with the same key. The old data and 
&gt;&gt;&gt; tombstones stay on disk until they happen to participate in compaction at 
&gt;&gt;&gt; the highest "level". The clean up can therefore happen days, weeks, or 
&gt;&gt;&gt; even months later depending upon the size of your dataset, speed of 
&gt;&gt;&gt; incoming writes, and distribution of new keys versus deleted keys.
&gt;&gt;&gt;
&gt;&gt;&gt; Basho has added code to leveldb in Riak 2.0 to more aggressively free up 
&gt;&gt;&gt; disk space. Details on this 2.0 feature are here:
&gt;&gt;&gt;
&gt;&gt;&gt; https://github.com/basho/leveldb/wiki/mv-aggressive-delete
&gt;&gt;&gt;
&gt;&gt;&gt; Matthew Von-Maszewski
