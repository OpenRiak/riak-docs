On Thu, Oct 17, 2013 at 10:45 AM, Sean Cribbs  wrote:

&gt; Since Jeremiah loves it when I'm pedantic, it bears mentioning that the
&gt; list of operations are rolled up immediately (not kept around), grouping by
&gt; which partition took the increment. So if I increment by 2 and then by 50,
&gt; and the increment goes to different replicas, my counter will look like
&gt; [{a, 2}, {b, 50}], for a sum of 52.
&gt;
&gt;
&gt; On Thu, Oct 17, 2013 at 4:21 PM, Jeremiah Peschka &lt;
&gt; jeremiah.pesc...@gmail.com&gt; wrote:
&gt;
&gt;&gt; When you 'update' a counter, you send in an increment operation. That's
&gt;&gt; added to an internal list in Riak. The operations are then zipped up to
&gt;&gt; provide the correct counter value on read. The worst that you'll do is add
&gt;&gt; a large(ish) number of values to the op list inside Riak.
&gt;&gt;
&gt;&gt; Siblings will be created, but they will not be visible to the end user
&gt;&gt; who is reading from the counter.
&gt;&gt;
&gt;&gt; Check out this demo of the new counter types from Sean Cribbs:
&gt;&gt; https://vimeo.com/43903960
