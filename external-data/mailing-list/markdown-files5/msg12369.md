&gt;&gt;&gt; David,
&gt;&gt;&gt; 
&gt;&gt;&gt; The standard way to kick transfers is setting transfer\\_limit to 0 and then 
&gt;&gt;&gt; back up to 2(default) or higher(up to 8 without turning other knobs). This 
&gt;&gt;&gt; can be done with `riak-admin transfer\\_limit 0` then `riak-admin 
&gt;&gt;&gt; transfer\\_limit 4`.
&gt;&gt;&gt; 
&gt;&gt;&gt; With that said, may I ask why you are upgrading nodes by leaving them and 
&gt;&gt;&gt; then re-joining them back to the cluster? Unless you are changing backend 
&gt;&gt;&gt; properties this should not be necessary and simply taking nodes down, 
&gt;&gt;&gt; upgrading them, and restarting them is the standard way to do a rolling 
&gt;&gt;&gt; upgrade[1].
&gt;&gt;&gt; 
&gt;&gt;&gt; Let us know how things go after kicking the transfers.
&gt;&gt;&gt; 
&gt;&gt;&gt; Thanks!
&gt;&gt;&gt; 
&gt;&gt;&gt; [1] http://docs.basho.com/riak/latest/ops/running/rolling-upgrades/
