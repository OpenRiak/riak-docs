It might also make a lot of sense to roll your own secondary indices. That
is, have a CRDT set represent the primary key of the rows which meet the 2i
condition. In that, you can query the CRDT set, and ensure some level of
consistency. There are further tricks to be played here if interested.

On Fri, Jul 22, 2016 at 11:01 AM, Alexander Sicular 
wrote:

&gt; Take a look at the "pw" and "pr" tunable consistency options for gets and
&gt; puts. The base level of abstraction in Riak is the virtual node - not the
&gt; physical machine. When data is replicated it is replicated to a replica set
&gt; of virtual nodes. Those virtual nodes have primary and secondary (due to
&gt; failures) allocations to physical machines. When using "pr" and "pw"
&gt; options you instruct Riak to only service the request from virtual nodes
&gt; that are residing on their primarily allocated physical machines. In short,
&gt; by abusing pr/pw you can infer the state of your cluster from your
&gt; application.
&gt;
&gt; Obviously, this is not foolproof. There may also be additional 2i specific
&gt; issues to consider. Nevertheless, I always liked this trick.
&gt;
