&gt;&gt;&gt;&gt; The major difference between these two is the first is executing a
&gt;&gt;&gt;&gt; binary, which has to basically create everything (connection, payload, etc)
&gt;&gt;&gt;&gt; every time through the loop. The second does not - it creates the client
&gt;&gt;&gt;&gt; once, then iterates over it keeping the same client and presumably the same
&gt;&gt;&gt;&gt; connection as well. That makes a huge difference.
&gt;&gt;&gt;&gt;
&gt;&gt;&gt;&gt; I would not use curl to do performance testing. What you probably want
&gt;&gt;&gt;&gt; is something like your python script that will work on many
&gt;&gt;&gt;&gt; threads/processes at once (or fire them up many times).
