On Sun, Jan 23, 2011 at 02:50:32PM -0500, Sean Cribbs wrote:
&gt; Anthony,
&gt; 
&gt; This is something I discovered a while back - define your backend names as 
&gt; binaries and you'll be able to set them properly from the REST interface. 
&gt; Example:
&gt; 
&gt; {storage\\_backend, riak\\_kv\\_multi\\_backend},
&gt; {multi\\_backend\\_default, &lt;&lt;"bitcask"&gt;&gt;},
&gt; {multi\\_backend,
&gt; [ {&lt;&lt;"bitcask"&gt;&gt;, riak\\_kv\\_bitcask\\_backend,
&gt; [{data\\_root, "/var/lib/riak/bitcask"}]},
&gt; {&lt;&lt;"dets"&gt;&gt;, riak\\_kv\\_dets\\_backend,
&gt; [{riak\\_kv\\_dets\\_backend\\_root, "/var/lib/riak/dets"}]},
&gt; {&lt;&lt;"ets"&gt;&gt;, riak\\_kv\\_ets\\_backend, []},
&gt; {&lt;&lt;"fs"&gt;&gt;, riak\\_kv\\_fs\\_backend,
&gt; [{riak\\_kv\\_fs\\_backend\\_root, "/var/lib/riak/fs"}]},
&gt; {&lt;&lt;"cache"&gt;&gt;, riak\\_kv\\_cache\\_backend,
&gt; [ {riak\\_kv\\_cache\\_backend\\_memory, 100},
&gt; {riak\\_kv\\_cache\\_backend\\_ttl, 600},
&gt; {riak\\_kv\\_cache\\_backend\\_max\\_ttl, 3600}
&gt; ]},
&gt; {&lt;&lt;"my\\_backend"&gt;&gt;, my\\_backend, []}
&gt; ]},
&gt; 
&gt; Then you can set it like so
&gt; 
&gt; curl -X PUT -H "content-type: application/json" -d 
&gt; '{"props":{"backend":"my\\_backend"}}' http://127.0.0.1:8098/riak/mybucket
&gt; 
&gt; Also note that only allow\\_mult and n\\_val are supported bucket properties from 
&gt; the PB interface (something we'll be fixing soon).
&gt;

&gt; Basho Technologies, Inc.
